# Energy, Economy & Carbon Emission Analytics Platform

## Overview

An end-to-end analytics project focused on understanding the relationship between carbon emissions, economic growth, population, and energy consumption using historical global climate data.

The project combines data analytics, business intelligence, forecasting, and sustainability analysis to generate actionable insights on global emissions trends.

## Objectives

* Analyze historical carbon emissions across countries.
* Study the relationship between GDP, population, energy consumption, and emissions.
* Identify major emission contributors.
* Compare country-level and per-capita emissions.
* Forecast future carbon emissions.
* Build interactive dashboards and analytics applications.

## Dataset

Source: Our World in Data (OWID)

Records: 50,000+ observations

Features:

* CO₂ Emissions
* GDP
* Population
* Energy Consumption
* Methane Emissions
* Greenhouse Gas Emissions
* Temperature Change Indicators

## Key Insights

* Global carbon emissions have increased significantly since industrialization.
* A small number of countries contribute a large share of global emissions.
* Economic growth and emissions show a positive relationship.
* Per-capita emissions provide a different perspective from total emissions.
* Energy consumption is strongly associated with carbon emissions.
